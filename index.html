01 The problem

Stateless models forget.
Characters shouldn't.

An LLM greets your player the same warm way whether they just confessed their love or burned the whole village. Prompts can fake a mood for one turn — they can't hold one.

A character with no inner life

  • — Resets to default every message. No grudges, no growth.
  • — Only "acts" an emotion if you re-paste the whole backstory.
  • — Treats a stranger and a soulmate identically.
  • — Non-deterministic vibes you can't test or trust.

A character that remembers how it feels

  • + Persistent affect per character, per relationship, per session.
  • + Bonds deepen, trust erodes, feelings linger and fade naturally.
  • + Emotional state shapes behavior — caution, warmth, repair.
  • + Deterministic & inspectable. Same history → same feeling.

That's why it's safe to ship: deterministic, inspectable, and impossible to leak raw state to the player.

03 How it works

Three calls. Any model. Real continuity.

Limbic sits beside your model as middleware. Send events, get back emotional context to prepend. That's the whole integration.

i.

Send what happened

Post the player's message and any world events to the character's session. Limbic appraises and updates persistent state.

ii.

Get the feeling

Receive a natural-language emotional_context block — and structured state for your own tooling.

iii.

Prepend & generate

Drop the block at the top of your prompt and call your model as usual. The character responds in-feeling.

integrate.ts
// 1 · tell Limbic what happened
const ctx = await limbic.guidance({
  agentId: "npc-zero-two",
  actorId: "kai",
  prompt: "I think your last fix was wrong — double-check it?"
});

// 2 · ctx.emotional_context is clean natural language
//    ## YOUR EMOTIONS … "underneath that, you also feel relieved, and determined."

// 3 · prepend & call ANY model
await openai.chat.completions.create({
  model: "gpt-4o",
  messages: [{ role: "system", content: ctx.emotional_context }, ...history]
});

      10 FAQ
      
Good questions.

    

    

      
Does this replace my LLM? 
No. Limbic sits beside it. You keep your model and prompts; Limbic supplies a natural-language emotional context block you prepend. It works with any model that takes a system message.

      
Is it deterministic? 
Yes. Given the same character profile and event history, Limbic produces the exact same affective state and the exact same injected text. No randomness, no wall-clock, no hidden network — so you can unit-test feelings in CI.

      
What does the model actually receive? 
Only natural language — a short ## YOUR EMOTIONS briefing. No raw numbers, policy fields, or JSON ever reach the prompt. All structured state stays server-side for your dashboards and balancing.

      
Can one character feel differently about different people? 
Absolutely — that's core. Each agent holds a separate relationship state per actor. The same line from a soulmate and from a stranger lands completely differently.

      
Are these "real" emotions? 
They're functional affect: deterministic operating-state signals that persist, compete, and shape behavior over time. Designed to make characters believable — not a claim of consciousness, sentience, or subjective experience.

      
How fast can I integrate? 
An afternoon. One call to get the emotional context, prepend it, generate. The live demo above is the real appraisal-and-render logic running in your browser.
